# 🏹 Hou Yi (后羿) — The Nine Suns Down

> *"The sky burned with ten suns. The earth cracked, the crops withered, and humanity faced total annihilation. Then came the Archer."*

This repository is dedicated to the ancient legend of **Hou Yi** (Da Yi), the mythical Chinese marksman who performed the ultimate act of celestial deicide.

---

## 📖 The Myth of the Sky on Fire

According to ancient Chinese mythology, the Eastern God Di Jun and the Goddess Xihe had ten children, each of whom was a **Sun God** manifested as a majestic three-legged crow. Normally, the mother goddess would drive her children across the sky one at a time, ensuring light and warmth for the mortal world below.

One day, bored of their strict routine, all ten suns decided to rise into the sky simultaneously.

The consequences were immediate and catastrophic:

* Rivers and oceans evaporated into steam.
* Vast forests erupted into unquenchable wildfires.
* The soil baked into stone, destroying all agriculture.
* Fierce mythical beasts crawled out of the dying wilderness to prey on starving humans.

Seeing the agony of mankind, the Emperor of Heaven gifted the mortal archer **Hou Yi** a divine red bow and a quiver of mystical white arrows.

---

## 🎯 The Slaying of the Suns

Hou Yi ascended the tallest mountains to face the blazing deities directly. He first attempted to reason with the suns, asking them to return to their normal rotation. When they ignored his warnings, he drew his bow.

With unmatched precision, Hou Yi shot his arrows into the heavens. One by one, the arrows pierced the hearts of the sun gods. As they died, they burst into brilliant plumes of golden feathers and plummeted to the earth, transforming back into giant, lifeless three-legged crows.

He systematically eliminated nine of the suns, plunging the world from a blinding white inferno back toward darkness.

Before he could pull his bowstring for the tenth and final time, the mortal rulers stopped him. They realized that without any sun at all, the earth would freeze into eternal night. Hou Yi spared the final sun, leaving it alone in the cosmos to provide the exact balance of light and heat that humanity needed to survive.

---

## 🏛️ Cultural Legacy

Hou Yi's myth remains a cornerstone of Chinese culture, directly tied to the origins of the **Mid-Autumn Festival** and his tragic separation from his wife, the Moon Goddess **Chang'e**. He stands in human history as one of the few mythical figures to strike down gods not out of malice or greed, but out of necessity—to save the world from an overwhelming, burning overload.
